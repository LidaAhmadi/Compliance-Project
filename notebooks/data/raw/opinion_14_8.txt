<p>DECISION &amp; JUDGMENT</p>
<p>Proceeding pursuant to CPLR article 78, inter alia, in the nature of prohibition to prohibit the respondent Anthony S. Senft, Jr., a Judge of the County Court, Suffolk County, from vacating the petitioner's plea agreement of February 6, 2024, in an action entitled <i>People v Beehler</i> , pending in the County Court, Suffolk County, under Indictment No. 72309/22, and, in effect, in the nature of mandamus to compel the respondent Anthony S. Senft, Jr., to sentence the petitioner in accordance with the plea agreement of February 6, 2024.</p>
<p>ADJUDGED that the petition is denied and the proceeding is dismissed on the merits, without costs or disbursements.</p>
<p>"Because of its extraordinary nature, prohibition is available only where there is a clear legal right, and then only when a court—in cases where judicial authority is challenged—acts or threatens to act either without jurisdiction or in excess of its authorized powers" (<i>Matter of Holtzman v Goldman</i> , <span class="citation" data-id="5538363"><a href="/opinion/5689144/holtzman-v-goldman/#569" aria-description="Citation for case: Holtzman v. Goldman">71 NY2d 564, 569</a></span>; <i>see Matter of Rush v Mordue</i> , <span class="citation" data-id="5537570"><a href="/opinion/5688391/rush-v-mordue/#352" aria-description="Citation for case: Rush v. Mordue">68 NY2d 348, 352</a></span>). The extraordinary remedy of mandamus will lie only to compel the performance of a ministerial act, and only where there exists a clear legal right to the relief sought (<i>see Matter of Legal Aid Socy. of Sullivan County v Scheinman</i> , <span class="citation" data-id="5533791"><a href="/opinion/5684901/legal-aid-society-of-sullivan-county-inc-v-scheinman/#16" aria-description="Citation for case: Legal Aid Society of Sullivan County, Inc. v. Scheinman">53 NY2d 12, 16</a></span>). The petitioner has failed to demonstrate a clear legal right to the relief sought.</p>
<p>The petitioner's remaining contentions are without merit.</p>
<p>DILLON, J.P., CHAMBERS, VENTURA and MCCORMACK, JJ., concur.</p>
